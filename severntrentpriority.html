Severn Trent Water | Priority Services

UX | Visual Design

Project Details

Type

Website Design

Date

April 26, 2016

Client

Severn Trent

Status

Completed

Severn Trent are constanlty pushing to help their customers in ways that exceed expectation. However, during Feburary 2016 Severn Trent experienced problems with their water supply and as a result some customers do not recieve the help they needed quickly because they were not registered to Severn Trent as customer who needs extra help.

Severn Trent approached us to design a web app for their customers to register themselves as a priority customer. This was fast paced project to design and build the web app in two weeks which could then be released to small number of real customer to test and provide feedback on the service.

THE FOCUS

We received valuable user research from the customer service team at Severn Trent which used to drive our user needs and design direction.

The current process of registering to the priority service envolves a user calling Sevren Trent and speaking with call center staff. This can be difficult for some users due to personal cercomstances and therefore missing call times.Our design solution was an interactive online form designed to streamline the registration process.

I didn't know I needed priority service until I realised I could leave my house.

One of the main reasons why some customers were not regustered with Severn Trent was that they did not realise they were eligiable for the priority service or were not aware the benifits of the service. This led us to look at how might we simplify the process of checking a users eligiabilty.

We started by breaking down the user journey of as-is process. We were able to astablish stucture of the form and start to create our to-be journey.

The use of coversational form patterns to make the online form a more natural and understandable process. A clean, stripped back visual style with the intergration of Severn Trent’s new design guidelines was used to make the form as simple as possible.

This was a responsive website to allow users to register on the move.
